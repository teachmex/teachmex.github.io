About Summer School, 2019

We are greatly excited with the new enthusiasm to introduce our Summer School in scientific computing in Python. The rapidly developing cloud computing infrastructure has facilitated parallel and distributed computing in very low cost. The quantity of data accumulated through simulation, lab experiments and other human activities has far exceeded the human capacity of manual curation and analysis, necessitating novel computing tools to extract the insights from large volumes of data/simulation. Our attempt in this winter school is to introduce the cutting age scientific computing tools and algorithms in Python.

We have developed a syllabus and projects to make participants familiar with fundamental of computing as well as standard methods in computing, including Python programming ( e.g., introduction to function, class and data-structure), numerical methods (e.g., solving differential equations), simulation (e.g., intoduction to Monte Carlo simulation), optimization( introduction to Machine Learning).

Our projects are designed in three major sectors of computing: differential equation, simulation and optimization. First project is based on configuration (boundary values), second project begins with mathematical model (Hamiltonian) to data creation (sampling) and last project takes data sample to create a mathematical model (classifier).
